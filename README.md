Global Supply Chain & Logistics Analytics :

Project Overview:
This project analyzes a global supply chain dataset containing over 180,000 records across orders, customers, products, shipping operations, and sales transactions.
The objective of this project was to identify delivery performance issues, profitability drivers, customer purchasing behavior, regional sales trends, and operational inefficiencies through data cleaning, KPI development, and interactive dashboard creation.

Key Business Insights:
1. High Delivery Delay Rate:
Approximately 55% of all deliveries were delayed, highlighting a significant opportunity to improve supply chain reliability and customer service levels.

2. Western Europe Drives Revenue but Faces Delivery Challenges:
Western Europe generated the highest sales and profit while also recording one of the highest delivery delay rates, making it a critical region for operational improvement.

3. Cleats Category Requires Attention:
The Cleats category ranked among the top categories in both sales and profit while also experiencing a high percentage of delayed deliveries, indicating potential inventory or logistics bottlenecks.

4. Heavy Discounts Did Not Improve Performance:
Products with lower discounts generated higher sales and profit than heavily discounted products, suggesting that aggressive discounting was not an effective growth strategy.

5. Corporate Customers Place Higher-Value Orders:
While Consumers generated the highest overall revenue, Corporate customers recorded the highest average order value, indicating strong potential for high-value sales opportunities.
